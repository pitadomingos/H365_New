# H365 Clinical User Manual

This manual provides step-by-step instructions for clinicians (Doctors, Nurses, Specialists) using the H365 platform.

## 1. Patient Intake & Triage
*   **Registration**: New patients must be registered via the **Patient Registration** module. Ensure a photo is captured for identity verification.
*   **Intake**: Returning patients should be processed in **Visiting Patients**. Search by National ID to add them to the daily waiting list.
*   **Vitals**: Initial vitals (BP, Temp, Weight) should be entered during intake. The system automatically interprets Blood Pressure status (e.g., Normal vs. Hypertensive Crisis).

## 2. Consultation Workflow (General & Specialist)
*   **Searching Patients**: Use the left-hand panel to view patients currently in your waiting list.
*   **AI Decision Support**: 
    1. Enter symptoms and current vitals.
    2. Click "Get AI Recommendation."
    3. Review the potential diagnoses and draft prescriptions.
    4. **Critical**: Clinicians must review and sign off on all AI suggestions. The AI is a supportive tool, not a diagnostic finality.
*   **Lab/Imaging Orders**: Click "Order Labs" or "Order Imaging" within the consultation form. Results will trigger a notification in your dashboard once ready.

## 3. Maternity Care (Antenatal)
*   **New Enrollment**: Log LMP (Last Menstrual Period). The system will automatically calculate the EDD (Estimated Date of Delivery) and current Gestational Age.
*   **Risk Tracking**: Use the "Risk Factors" section to flag high-risk pregnancies (e.g., Hypertension, History of C-Section).
*   **Follow-up**: Every antenatal visit should include FHR (Fetal Heart Rate) and Fundal Height monitoring.

## 4. Pediatrics & Child Wellness (PAV)
*   **Immunization**: View the automated vaccination schedule based on the child's age. Log the batch number and date for every dose administered.
*   **Growth Charts**: Regularly log Weight and Height. Use the MUAC (Mid-Upper Arm Circumference) field to screen for malnutrition.

## 5. Chronic Disease Management (ART/TB)
*   **Adherence**: Use the **DOTS Log** to record daily medication intake for TB patients.
*   **Clinical Monitoring**: Monitor Viral Load and CD4 trends via the visual charts to ensure treatment efficacy.

## 6. Ward Management (In-Patient)
*   **Admissions**: Move patients from the "Pending Admission" list to specific beds in medical, surgical, or maternity wards.
*   **Medication Schedules**: Nurses should update the medication schedule in real-time as doses are administered.

## 7. Emergency & Operation Theatre (OT)
*   **OT Safety**: Before any procedure, the **WHO Surgical Safety Checklist** must be completed digitally (Sign-In, Time-Out, Sign-Out).
*   **Emergency Triage**: Use the ER Status dashboard to prioritize critical "Red" alerts.
